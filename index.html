Our Story

Luna Bella got her love for food and cooking from her grandmother, Electra. At a young age, Electra began teaching her how to cook with the traditional Italian recipes she'd grown up with in Florence, Italy. Electra was born and raised in Italy, moving to the U.S. in her mid-30s, so she passed on not only the recipes she loved but also stories of life there, and Luna Bella fell in love with all of it.

After graduating high school, Luna Bella moved to Italy to pursue a culinary education and to get closer to the types of food her grandmother taught her to make. She finished her education and spent another 10 years working and learning in Rome. It was during this time that she met Constantino and they became fast friends who decided to open a restaurant together in America in order to share genuine Italian recipes with everyone.
